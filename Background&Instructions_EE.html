Background and Instructions for Experts

Author

Emily Burton

Background

Defined Problem

What home tick control and personal protective methods are appropriate for a New England resident, given how much they may value efficacy vs. environmental impact vs. expenses?

Decision Maker: Resident

Decision Class: Multi objective problem (multi criteria decision)

  • Need to evaluate alternatives performance of multiple objectives, then scale to each other

  • Often no exact data for each alternative

Brief Description of Decision Making Tool

To help NE residents choose the best tick control and personal protective measures for them, we will collect data on the performance of multiple alternatives (commonly used tick control and personal protective methods) at different objectives (goals when problem solving, i.e., increase efficacy or minimize costs).

The user of the tool can then weigh the different objectives according to their personal values and beliefs to find the alternatives that perform best at what they care about. For instance, someone that wants to minimize tick populations and encounters with ticks regardless of the cost may need to employ a different strategy than someone who cares about efficacy but does not want to spend much money or time on tick control and bite prevention.

Role of Expert Elicitation

Given the lack of empirical data for alternatives performance of different objectives, and the difficulty in comparing existing empirical data collected in studies that can vary greatly in methods and context, the most consistent source of data to fill this decision making tool is expert opinion.

Experts are individuals that have published at least [X] studies on tick control and tick personal protect measures within the last 10 years, thus implying they have extensive knowledge of the current state of knowledge in the field. We will be pooling data from multiple experts, which allows us to evaluate where there is consensus and where opinions vary greatly.

Objectives

Objectives = goals when addressing the problem

Measurement = a means to evaluate an alternatives performance of an objective in a quantitative or qualitative manner

ID Objective Measurement Scale Sources
O1 Minimize tick populations at home Ticks collected / hour dragging. 0 — 150 ticks / hr Scale based on ITCH data of tick collection in RI residents yards
O2 Minimize encounters at home Ticks encountered (i.e., found attached or crawling) / hour in yard Scale: 0 — 50
O3 Minimize negative environmental impacts (i.e., damage to the ecosystem and loss of non-target species) Negative impact on environment Scale: 0 — 100 (likert scale)

0 = No negative impact
25 = Minimal negative impact impact
50 = Moderate negative impact
75 = Major negative impact
100 = Environment completely negatively impacted
O4 Minimize financial costs Cost of tick control or personal protective measure in USD for a single tick active season. Scale: $0 — \geq$5000 USD Scale based on Schulze et al. 2023
O5 Minimize time spent Hours required to effectively deploy tick control or personal protective measure during active tick season (includes labor hours for paid work) Scale: 0 - 72hrs

Instructions for Experts

Experts will provide three values for the dataset for each alternative \times objective combination (300 total values), a confidence estimate for each combination, and will have opportunities to provide additional information for each combination.

For each alternative \times objective combination, we would like experts to envision an average NE residence or resident, where an alternative has the best chance of working. In our end tool we will have users filter out alternatives irrelevant to them or their property (e.g., remove deer fencing as an option for those with non-forested properties, and pet treatments for those without outdoor pets).

Input Values

To capture uncertainty in expert estimates, we would like experts to provide us with a range for their mean estimate of an alternatives performance of an objective (using the measurement provided), as well as an estimate of the mean.

  • Minimum of Range: The lowest value you think the mean could be
  • Maximum of Range: The highest value you think the mean could be
  • Mean: Within the range, what you think the mean actually is

Note: We are not asking for you to provided the full range of potential values for a measurement (i.e., the lowest number of ticks collected at a property with no tick control methods is 0, but the lowest estimate of the average number of ticks collected would be higher), but a range of your estimate of the mean. Additionally, we do not expect the data to be normally distributed, so your point estimate of the mean does not need to be centered within the range.

Confidence Estimates + Additional Information

To better understand the values an expert provides, as well as the sources of uncertainty within the data collected, we would like experts to characterize their confidence in their estimates for each alternative \times objective combination on a percent scale, where 0 is completely not confident and 100 is completely confident.

We then have provided a box for experts to explain why they may lack confidence (or have very high confidence) in an estimate. We are particularly interested in knowing if a lack of confidence derives from unfamiliarity with a particular combination, or a general lack of data on a combination.

We provided an additional box for experts to provide any other comments or questions on an alternative x objective combination.
